Casting & Charges

Two synergy types change how a psycast fires rather than how hard it hits: multi-target casting and ability charges. This page also covers how per-add-on scaling is applied.

Multi-target & area

A skill whose synergy type is Targets can be made to strike additional targets - as its feeding mates level, the target count rises, turning a single strike into a small volley. Area abilities likewise gain blast radius from the Area type.

Target count
targets = baseTargets + ⌊ Σ Lsrc · rate ⌋
AI limitation: the enemy/automatic AI only auto-casts single-target psycasts, so AI casters never fire the multi-target version on their own. Player-directed casts use the full count.

Charges

The Charges synergy type is special - synergy-only, never a primary. A charged ability banks extra casts before it must cool down, so feeding mates let a psycaster chain a burst. Charges are counted directly from source levels, so the tooltip shows a real count:

Charges per feeding mate
charges = ⌊ 0.5 · Lsrc · strength ⌋

Per-add-on scaling

Psycasts² reads whatever psycast mods you have and applies its scaling generically. For add-ons with bespoke ability shapes (custom projectiles, summons, beams, orbital effects) it carries targeted compatibility so their unique mechanics scale sensibly too. A handful of exotic effects (certain summon counts, shield geometry, beam fire-radius) are noted as in-progress in the Field Manual.

Cost & cooldown

Every cast carries a psyfocus cost and a cooldown, which is why Cost and Cooldown are the universal synergy fallbacks - any skill can receive them. Note that leveling a skill never reduces its psyfocus cost (cost is intentionally unaffected by levels); cost relief comes only from synergies, specializations, and the unaffected base. See Psycaster Stats for the cost-factor rule.
