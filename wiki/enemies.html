Enemy Psycasters

If you can transcend, so can your enemies. Hostile psycasters - especially the Empire - can spawn already Enlightened: tiered, with extra schools and a boosted psylink, and on the rarest rolls an ascension of their own.

Tier roll

On spawn, an eligible hostile psycaster rolls for an enlightenment tier, weighted toward the lower tiers. Only tiers you have left enabled are eligible.

TierRarityExtra schoolsBonus psylink levels
I - AwakenedCommon+1+3
II - EnlightenedUncommon+2+7
III - AscendedRare+3+12

Extra schools are granted in tier order (low first) so each ability's prerequisites are already met, and the psylink is raised to power them. The tier hediff is the same one your colonists carry, so a captured enemy psycaster keeps their enlightenment.

Enemy ascension

At Tier III, a rare further roll can grant the enemy one of the seven ascension constellations - aura and all. A fully-realised hostile Ascendant is a genuine boss-tier threat. This is gated behind its own setting, off by default.

Enemy tiers scale the danger of psychic raids considerably. The Enemies settings tab lets you cap the maximum tier, tune the rarity, and disable the rare enemy ascension independently.

Why it matters

Player power in Psycasts² grows steeply - a leveled, specialized, Transcendent caster is formidable. Enlightened enemies keep psychic warfare a two-sided arms race instead of a one-sided power fantasy, so late-game raids stay tense.
