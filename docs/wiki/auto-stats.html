Psycaster Stats

Vanilla makes you spend points to upgrade neural heat, recovery and sensitivity. Psycasts² grows those three automatically every level - freeing your points for skill levels - and scales the growth with your enlightenment tier. Psyfocus cost is left untouched.

How psycaster levels are earned

A psycaster's level rises with experience from casting psycasts - and a meditation breakthrough grants a whole level at once. Each level costs more XP than the last: the increment grows 15% per level to 20, then 10% to 30, then 5% beyond - so leveling slows sharply past 30.

Example: level 2 costs ~100 XP, level 20 ~1,400, level 30 ~3,700, level 50 ~9,800. That steep late curve is exactly why the breakthrough chance curves up with tier - a free level is worth far more at high level than at low - and why the level cap only opens past 30 as you tier up, so those costly levels are banked at the higher per-level rate.

Each stat row in Modern Psycasts UI's left panel shows the running total our growth has contributed, as a right-aligned (+N). Hover the row for a breakdown of the per-level gain at each enlightenment tier, with your current tier marked. The manual "Upgrade - Psycaster Stats" button is replaced by a note explaining the automation.

Sensitivity is deliberately a gentle +0.25% per level - it stacks on top of the flat tier bonuses (+10 / +20 / +35%) and any Apotheosis's sensitivity, so a small per-level drip keeps the total in check.

Tier scaling - non-retroactive

The growth rate climbs 15% per enlightenment tier. Each level is banked at the rate of the tier you held when you earned it - reaching a higher tier never rewrites past levels, it only speeds up future ones.

Accumulator (once, on each new level)
stat += base · RateMult(tiernow) RateMult(tier) = 1 + 0.15 · tier
basethe stat's tier-0 per-level amount (15 / 0.0625 / 0.05)
tiernowthe pawn's enlightenment tier when the level is gained

Worked example

A pawn reaches level 10 while unawakened (Tier 0), Awakens (Tier I), then earns levels 11-15. Neural-heat-limit offset:

Non-retroactive accumulation
levels 1-10 at ×1.00: 10 · 15 · 1.00 = 150 levels 11-15 at ×1.15: 5 · 15 · 1.15 = 86.25 ⇒ total offset = 236.25 # vs 258.75 if it were retroactive
The difference is the point. 236.25 vs 258.75 is exactly the early levels declining to be re-valued at the new tier - that is the non-retroactive rule, so points earned at higher tiers are permanently worth more.

Seeing non-retroactive scaling

Follow a pawn from level 1 all the way to the Tier-10 cap (level 150), tiering up every 12 levels. The blue line is the heat offset they actually accumulate; the gold line is what they would have if every past level were re-valued at the current tier. The two are identical until the first tier-up, then the gold pulls steadily ahead - by Tier 10 it is ~50% higher. That entire gap is the early levels Psycasts² does not rewrite.

Another way to see it: each tier's levels are a separate deposit. The bar's width is how many levels that tier lasts; its height is the per-level rate; so the area of each block is the stat it banks. Tiering up only sets the height of the block you are currently filling - it never reshapes a finished one. The rate climbs the whole way, from ×1.00 at Tier 0 to ×2.50 at the Tier-10 ceiling.

Across the full climb to level 150 (this scenario) the banked auto-stats total roughly +3,735 neural heat, +15.6/s recovery, and +62% sensitivity - on top of the flat tier sensitivity (+35%) and any Apotheosis. Retroactive scaling would have inflated all three by ~50%; non-retroactive is what keeps the deep endgame in check.

Who it applies to

  • Tracked pawns (your awakened colonists) use the accumulator. The first time it runs it seeds existing levels at the Tier-0 rate, so loading a save never shifts your current stats.
  • Untracked psycasters (enemies, colonists before Awakening) use the flat Tier-0 rate - identical to a simple level × base, with no per-pawn bookkeeping stored.

The whole feature is one toggle (Auto psycaster stats); turning it off restores vanilla manual stat upgrades.
